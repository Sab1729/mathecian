Chapter 2: Working with nicer relations

In some relations, like assigning a number to a person, we can't really simplify things. Say:

1 to Bob, 2 to David, 3 to Charlie, 4 to Alice (You’ll find these people everywhere!)

There’s no neat way to write this without just listing everything. Each time, we’d have to write out the entire relation from scratch. Since that requires patience (which we don’t have) and looks boring (which we don’t like), we shall not discuss them.

Instead, let’s look at relations like these:

1 to 2, 2 to 4, 3 to 6, 4 to 8

Remember we wrote this as $a$ to $2a$ relation (yes-yes, given that $av\in \{1,2,3,4\}$ ).
If we look at the skeleton of how we express such relations, it usually follows this structure

For $a \in A$ relate $a$ to (something done to $a$)

In our case the “something done” was to double $a$. That is why we wrote $2 \times a$
